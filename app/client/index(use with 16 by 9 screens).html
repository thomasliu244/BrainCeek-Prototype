BrainCeek Instructions

Section 1: Finding Transactions

1. Go to capitaliq.com, click the screening button to start looking for your precedent transactions

2. Select Transactions, since you are trying to filter and find the right M&A transactions to compare with a potential sale of a retail company (i.e. Nike)

3. The first filter we want to use is dates – we want relatively recent deals and not ones from 1997

4. Select “Announced Date”, adjust to “11/28/16 to 11/28/18”, and click “Add Criteria”

5. Moving on to the second filter, since the size of the deal also matters (and Capital IQ has many erroneous deals below $10mm), click “Valuations” to start filtering for those”

6. After highlighting “Transaction Valuations / Multiples”, click on “Total Transaction Value.” Make sure to input “10” in the value box and click “Add Criteria”

8. Be sure to select United States of America, and not United States and Canada. Then click on “Add Criteria”

9. Finally, select Industry Classifications to further filter for the deals which fit into Nike’s current industry

10. After clicking on Apparel and Footwear, be sure to click “Add Criteria”

11. Click on “View Results” to reveal the 24 deals which Capital IQ has generated

12. Click on “Customize Display Columns” to adjust the outputs and fields shown for each specific deal
